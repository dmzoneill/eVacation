Annual Leave

Applies to: All Employees who’s Leave Tracking is enabled within e-Vacation.e-Vacation is designed primarily for Shannon employees, therefore, the leave rules are based on those specific to Intel Shannon. For more details on these entitlements please review information on Circuit or contact your e-Vacation Administrator.

Within e-Vacation, an employee’s Total Annual Leave Entitlement (i.e. vacation) is calculated as follows:

Annual Leave OR Total Entitlement =
Basic Entitlement + Carry Over (from previous year) + Exceptions (rounded to next half day).

e-Vacation captures all leave data already recorded in employee’s vacation entitlement log since 1 January 2006 onwards.
Basic Entitlement

Applies to: All Employees

Rule: 25 working days (i.e. Monday to Friday) per year, pro-rated if joining the company during the year.

To be Taken: Anytime between 2 January(1 January is Public Holiday) and 31 December of the same year. If vacation days are not taken by 31 December of the same year they will be lost (refer to Carry Over and Exceptions rules below).

e-Vacation will calculate entitlement pro-rated if joining or leaving the company during the year; Vacation can be booked/taken at a steady rate over the year or in bulk, in advance as appropriate and as approved with manager.
Carry over end of year (EOY)

Applies to: Exempts only

Rule: If an employee does not use all his holidays in a year the unused days will be entered by an administrator as carry over for the following year and will be taken as vacation days.

To be Taken: Anytime between 2 January(1 January is Public Holiday) and 31 March of the same year. If the number of days carried-over are not taken by 31 March of the same year, they will be lost.

Acquired: They are acquired from the previous years entitlement and booked/taken as annual leave (up to March 31) with management approval. The employee’s Carried Over entitlement for a specified year is entered into the e-Vacation by an e-Vacation administrator.

For Example,
Joe Bloggs worked from 2nd of January (1st January is a Public Holidays) to 31 Dec 2005 and had only taken 11 holidays (for simplicity Joe only has a Basic Entitlement in 2005 of 25 days in this example and has taken all of this).

    25 - 15
    = 10 days Carry Over for 2006

On 31 Dec 2005 an e-Vacation Administrator enters 10 days Carry Over EOY for Joe in 2006.

On 2 Jan 2006 Joe has
      25 days Basic Entitlement
    + 10 days Carry Over (from previous year)
    = 35 days Total Entitlement.
    ________________________________
    35 days Available Balance

However, by 31 March 2006, Joe has only taken 8 days Annual Leave.

On 31 March 2006 Joe has:

    35 days Available Balance
   - 8 Days Booked
    ________________________________
    23 days Available Balance

On 1 April 2006, an e-Vacation administrator will remove the carried over days that have not been taken by 31 March 2006.
On 2 April 2006 Joe has:
      25 days Basic Entitlement
    + 8 days Carry Over (from previous year)
    = 33 days Total Entitlement.
    ________________________________
    33 days Available Balance

    - 8 Days Booked
    ________________________________
    25 days Available Balance
Exceptions

Applies to: All employees

Rule : This field will be used to adjust specific situations. For clarification about calculation, please contact e-vacation administrator.

To be Taken: Anytime between 2 January(1 January is Public Holiday) and 31 December of the same year. If vacation days are not taken by 31 Dec of the same year they will be lost.

Acquired: They can be booked/taken once acquired. They can be taken combined with other vacation days or taken in bulk later in the year.

For Example,
In Year 2000, Joe Bloggs arranged to have 5 days Carry Over to 2001, 3 days to 2002 and 2 days to 2003 (for simplicity Joe only has a Basic Entitlement of 26 days in this example).

An e-Vacation Administrator has entered the following exceptional entitlements for Joe:

    5 Exceptional days to year 2001
    3 Exceptional days to year 2002
    2 Exceptional days to year 2003

On 2 Jan 2001 Joe has:
      26 days Basic Entitlement
    + 5 Exceptional days
    = 31 days Total Entitlement.
    ________________________________
    31 days Available Balance

On 2 Jan 2002 Joe has:
      26 days Basic Entitlement
    + 3 Exceptional days
    = 29 days Total Entitlement.
    ________________________________
    29 days Available Balance

On 2 Jan 2003 Joe has:
      26 days Basic Entitlement
    + 2 Exceptional days
    = 28 days Total Entitlement.
    ________________________________
    28 days Available Balance
Extended Leave Program

Applies to: All employees.

Rule: An employee who wants to join ELP has to submit a request on line (this has to be done outside of e-vacation) via the process described on the Circuit website. Upon receipt of the request, the e-Vacation Administrator enters in the “ELP” tab of the tool:

  • the ELP activation date
  • the total number of days (25 days) to be banked across the entire ELP period
  • the 5 days banked at opening


Matured ELP To be Taken: Once an activated ELP has “matured”, it can be booked / taken between the date it matures to the date it expires.

Acquired: ELP vacations can be booked/taken once the matured ELP is available to take.

Activating an ELP:
On activation, employee must have enough “Total Entitlement Balance” to be transferred to ELP.     Days Banked On Activation = 5 days
    Total Days to be Banked = 25 days

Once ELP is activated, 5 days will be banked per year, transferred on January 1st each year until ELP matures.

ELP Relief:
During ELP active lifetime, should an employee not want to transfer vacation days over to ELP for a particularly year or years, ELP relief can be granted for a specified year and entered by an e-Vacation Administrator.

Matured ELP:
ELP maturity date is calculated on the 5th anniversary of opening, plus any relief given if applicable.

    example
14 April 2004: ELP activation - 5 days banked
14 April 2005: 10 days banked
14 April 2006: 15 days banked
14 April 2007: 20 days banked
14 April 2008: 25 days banked

From April 15, 2008, the employee is entitled to take his leave.

    Expected Years To Mature = 5 Years To Mature

    Maturity Date = 5th anniversary day of maturation + 1 day

ELP Expiration:
A matured ELP has to be taken in a maximum of 5 years from maturation date.
Example:
ELP matures on April 14th 2008
Expiration date: April 14th 2013

Booking matured ELP:
When booking / taking ELP, the full amount of days (25 days) has to be taken as one ELP leave period. The employee can also add normal vacation days to the extended leave period.
The employee must submit his leave request to his manager at least 3 months in advance in order to plan the leave.

Closure of ELP:
At any time an employee can choose to close his ELP account. The banked days are integrated back into the pool of the “normal” vacation to be taken by end of calendar year. Upon closure of an ELP prior to maturation, re-opening an ELP is subject to HR approval (EMEA C&B manager).
In very specific cases (e.g. wedding – detailed list available from the ELP details on Employee Services), ELP can be paid out. For these specific cases, the employee can re-start an ELP at any time.
For both cases the employee should notify HR of this decision by letter. From this date no more days will be banked in the account.
Part time vacation calulations

Applies to: Part-Time employees.

Rule: The number of basic entitlements granted to a Part-Time employee is the same as for Full-Time employees. However, when a Part-Time employee raises a Leave Request, the rules to calculate the number of vacation " used " are the following :

     The number of days corresponding to the leave request are based on the ones usually
     worked within Intel Shannon and not on the days normally worked by the Part-Time employee

     The first day to consider for the calculation corresponds to the first day the Part-Time
     employee would have worked.

    The last day to consider for the calculation corresponds to the last day usually worked within
     Intel Shannon, even if it doesn't correspond to a day normally worked by the Part-Time
     employee.

A manual adjustment will be made quarterly (if required) to the Leave Balance of Part-Time employees, to ensure these legal requirement are applied.

Examples

Thomas works on Mondays, Tuesdays, Thursdays, Fridays
 He raises a request:
    From Monday to Friday : the request will count for 5 days
    From Thursday to Friday : the request will count for 2 days
    For Monday (considering Tuesday is a bank holiday): the request will count for 2 days
    (Monday + Wednesday)

Sophie works on Tuesdays, Wednesdays, Thursdays and Fridays :
 She raises a request :<
    From Monday to Friday: the request will count for 4 days
    From Friday to the next Friday : the request will count for 6 days
    For Friday (considering the next Tuesday is a bank holiday) : the request will count for 2 days
    (Friday + Monday)
Is Employee Leave Tracked?

Is the employee Blue Badge AND Entity Code = 501 AND Active this Year? Then YES.

An employee who is classified as “Active This Year” is an employee who a) is not terminated and b) the year this employee started (i.e. Last Date Of Hire) is not in a future year.
